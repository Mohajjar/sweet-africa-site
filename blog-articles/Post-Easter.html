The Post-Easter Reality Check: How to Actually Clean Up After the Holidays

The Easter baskets are empty, the ham is reduced to leftovers, and your home looks like a pastel-colored tornado hit it. Chocolate fingerprints decorate your upholstery, plastic Easter grass clings to every surface like glitter’s evil cousin, and—why does the house still smell like deviled eggs?

You need a cleanup strategy that’s fast, effective, and doesn’t require a miracle. Here’s how to tackle the post-Easter mess without losing your sanity.

1. The Chocolate Stain Triage

Why It’s Worse Than You Think: Chocolate + sunlight + fabric = permanent stains if not treated fast.

  • On Fabric: Scrape off excess, then dab with cold water + dish soap (hot water sets the stain).
  • On Carpets: Mix 1 tbsp white vinegar + 1 tbsp dish soap + 2 cups warm water. Blot—don’t scrub!
  • On Wood: Wipe with a dry microfiber cloth first to avoid smearing, then use a drop of olive oil to buff out residue.

Pro Tip: Keep a stain-removal pen in your junk drawer for next year’s candy emergencies.

2. The Plastic Grass Apocalypse

Why It’s Everywhere: Static cling turns those shiny strands into home decor.

  • Vacuum Hack: Put pantyhose over the hose end to catch grass without clogging.
  • Lint Roller Trick: Roll over upholstery and rugs to pick up strays.
  • Prevent Next Year: Use shredded paper or fabric strips instead—just as pretty, way less evil.

3. The Lingering “Ham Smell” Situation

Science Says: Fat particles from cooking cling to walls, curtains, and even ceilings.

  • Simmer Pot: Boil citrus peels + cinnamon sticks + cloves to neutralize odors.
  • Vodka Spritz: Mix 1 part vodka + 2 parts water in a spray bottle. Mist fabrics (yes, even curtains).
  • Bowl of Coffee Grounds: Leave overnight to absorb stubborn smells.

4. The Hidden Candy Wrapper Hunt

Where Kids Stash Them:

  • Under couch cushions
  • In plant pots (why??)
  • Between bed sheets (gross)

Make It a Game:

  • “Treasure Hunt” Challenge: Offer a prize for the most wrappers collected.
  • Vacuum with a Flashlight: Shine it along baseboards to spot shiny foil.

5. The Post-Brunch Dish Mountain

The Lazy Person’s Approach:

  1. Soak Everything: Fill pots/pans with hot water + dish soap and walk away.
  2. Load the Dishwasher Strategically: Bowls on bottom, plates on top, and skip pre-rinsing (modern dishwashers can handle it).
  3. Hand-Wash Only the Essentials: One mug, one plate, one fork—everything else can wait.

The 20-Minute “Good Enough” Reset

  • Wipe one counter (so you have coffee space tomorrow).
  • Hide clutter in a laundry basket (out of sight = out of mind).
  • Light a candle (fake it till you make it).
  • Close the doors to messy rooms (no shame).
