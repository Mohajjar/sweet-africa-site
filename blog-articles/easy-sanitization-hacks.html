Easy Sanitization Hacks for Highland’s Small Businesses


Running a small business in Highland’s busy retail scene comes with a challenge: keeping your space sanitized. With 56,400 residents flocking to local shops and eateries, high-touch surfaces like counters, door handles, and restrooms can become germ hotspots. A clean store isn’t just about looks—it builds customer trust and keeps staff healthy. Don’t have hours to deep clean? Here are three eco-friendly sanitization hacks you can do in minutes, plus a pro tip to make your Highland business shine.

Hack 1: Castile Soap for Sparkling Counters

High foot traffic in Highland’s strip malls leaves counters dusty and sticky. Grab liquid castile soap, a plant-based cleaner safe for all surfaces. Mix one tablespoon with a quart of warm water in a spray bottle. Spritz your counters, cash registers, and display cases, then wipe with a microfiber cloth. It cuts through grime without harsh chemicals, perfect for the 30% of Inland Empire customers who love green cleaning. Do this daily for a spotless first impression.

Hack 2: Tea Tree Oil Spray for Door Handles

Door handles and light switches are germ magnets in busy shops. Make a natural disinfectant with tea tree oil, known for its antibacterial power. Add 10 drops to a cup of water in a small spray bottle. Shake well, then mist handles, card readers, and restroom faucets. Wipe with a clean cloth. This quick trick, done twice daily, keeps germs at bay and leaves a fresh scent customers notice.

Hack 3: Daily Cloth Refresh for Restrooms

Restrooms in Highland businesses see heavy use, and reusable cloths can spread germs if not cleaned. After wiping down sinks and toilets with your castile soap mix, toss cloths in a bucket of hot water with a splash of vinegar. Soak for 10 minutes, then machine-wash. This ensures fresh cloths daily, reducing odors and health risks. It’s a simple step that makes your restroom feel welcoming.

Why These Hacks Work

These solutions are fast, affordable, and eco-friendly, fitting the needs of Highland’s small businesses. Castile soap and tea tree oil are non-toxic, protecting staff and customers, while the cloth refresh saves money on disposables. Together, they tackle the 20% rise in commercial cleaning demand (IBISWorld, 2025) without eating into your day.
